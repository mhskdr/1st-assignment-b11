Vision and Challenges in New Bangladesh

Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

The idea of Bangladesh 2.0 is to create a new Bangladesh with new ideas and innovations.

Sustainability

Innovation is the key to success. We need to innovate in every sector to create a new Bangladesh.

Infrastructure

We need to have a clear vision for the future of Bangladesh. We need to set our goals and work towards them.

Empowerment

There will be challenges on the way to creating a new Bangladesh. We need to face them and overcome them.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nations trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"Bangladesh has the potential to become a global leader in sustainable development. By harnessing the power of innovation and technology, we can create a brighter future for all.The direct elimination of poverty should be the objective of all development aid.Development should be viewed as a human rights issue, not as a question of simply increasing the gross national product GNP. If we are looking for one single action which will enable the poor to overcome their poverty, I would focus on credit"

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Bangladesh Launches First Satellite

Date:11/01/2024 Time: 06:25 AM

Bangladesh has launched its first satellite, marking a significant milestone in the country's space program. The satellite, named Bangabandhu-1, was launched from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, USA, on May 11, 2018. The satellite will provide telecommunications and broadcasting services to rural areas in Bangladesh, helping to bridge the digital divide and improve access to information and communication technologies.
